Types of Storm Damage We Repair in Lynchburg

Storm damage to roofs takes many forms, and identifying the exact type of damage is critical to performing repairs that last. Virginia Roof Pro’s experienced inspectors are trained to identify and document all of the following:

  • Hail Damage: Hailstones leave circular impact marks, bruising, and granule loss on asphalt shingles. Even small hailstones can compromise the waterproofing layer of your roof, leading to premature aging and leaks months later.
  • Wind Damage: High winds peel back shingles, break the seal strips, and lift flashing away from chimneys, skylights, and roof edges. Wind-lifted shingles allow water to penetrate the decking immediately.
  • Fallen Tree & Debris Damage: Trees along the James River and the wooded hillsides above Blackwater Creek Trail can fall during storm events. Impact damage from fallen trees or large limbs often requires structural repairs in addition to roofing work.
  • Water Intrusion & Leak Damage: Ice dams, compromised valleys, and saturated decking from extended rain events require prompt attention to prevent mold, rot, and structural deterioration.
  • Gutter & Fascia Damage: Storms frequently damage gutters and fascia boards, compromising water drainage away from your foundation and creating secondary damage risks.
