What It Means to Be a Licensed Roofing Contractor in Virginia

The Commonwealth of Virginia requires roofing contractors to hold an active Class A, B, or C contractor license issued by the Virginia Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation (DPOR). This process is far from trivial. To obtain and maintain this license, a contractor must pass a trade knowledge examination, demonstrate financial solvency, provide proof of liability insurance and workers’ compensation coverage, and adhere to ongoing continuing education requirements.

Virginia Roof Pro holds an active Virginia contractor license and we encourage every Lynchburg homeowner to verify any roofing company’s credentials through the DPOR public lookup before signing a contract. This simple step protects your home, your wallet, and — importantly — your legal standing should anything go wrong during or after a repair.

Licensing vs. Simply “Being in Business”

Many roofing outfits operating in Lynchburg and the surrounding Piedmont region carry business registrations without holding a valid DPOR contractor license. A business registration alone does not qualify a company to perform structural roofing work in Virginia. Only a licensed contractor has been vetted by the state to meet safety, insurance, and competency standards. When you choose Virginia Roof Pro, you’re choosing a contractor whose qualifications have been independently verified — not just self-proclaimed.

Frequently Asked Questions — Licensed Roof Repair in Lynchburg, VA

How do I verify a roofing contractor is licensed in Virginia?

You can verify any roofing contractor’s Virginia license through the Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation (DPOR) public database at dpor.virginia.gov. Simply search by the company name or license number. Virginia Roof Pro is fully licensed and we encourage all Lynchburg homeowners to confirm our credentials before hiring. A valid license number, active status, and no disciplinary history are the key things to look for.

Why is it important to hire a licensed roof repair contractor in Lynchburg?

Hiring a licensed contractor protects you in several critical ways. Licensed contractors carry liability insurance and workers’ compensation coverage — shielding you from financial responsibility if something goes wrong. They are qualified to pull permits for work that requires city approval in Lynchburg. They are also held to Virginia’s building code standards and can face license revocation for substandard work. Unlicensed roofers offer none of these protections, and faulty work can void your homeowner’s insurance coverage.

Does a roof repair in Lynchburg require a building permit?

It depends on the scope of the work. Minor repairs such as patching a few shingles or sealing flashing typically do not require a permit in Lynchburg. However, more extensive repairs — such as replacing large sections of decking, structural work, or anything affecting the integrity of the roofing system — may require a permit from the City of Lynchburg’s Building Inspections department. Only a licensed contractor is authorized to legally pull a building permit in Virginia. Virginia Roof Pro handles all permit procurement when required.

What types of insurance should a licensed Lynchburg roofer carry?

A reputable licensed roofer in Lynchburg should carry at minimum two types of insurance: general liability insurance (to cover property damage during the repair process) and workers’ compensation insurance (to cover medical costs if a worker is injured on your property). Without workers’ comp, a homeowner can potentially be held liable for a worker’s injury. Virginia Roof Pro maintains both types of coverage and will gladly provide certificates of insurance before any work begins.

How much does licensed roof repair typically cost in Lynchburg, VA?

The cost of licensed roof repair in Lynchburg varies based on several factors — the size and extent of the damage, the roofing material involved (asphalt shingles, metal, slate, etc.), accessibility of the roof, and whether any underlying decking has been compromised. Minor repairs for a few shingles or a small leak seal might range from $300 to $600, while more complex repairs involving flashing, decking, or valleys can run $800 to $2,500 or more.
