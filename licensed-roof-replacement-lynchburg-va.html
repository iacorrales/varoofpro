What It Means to Be a Licensed Roofing Contractor in Virginia

In Virginia, residential and commercial contractors must obtain a valid license through the Virginia Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation (DPOR). Roofing falls under the state’s contractor licensing framework, with Class A, Class B, and Class C licenses dictating the scope and dollar value of work a contractor is legally authorized to perform. A Class A license is required for projects exceeding $120,000; Class B covers projects between $10,000 and $120,000; Class C applies to smaller projects under $10,000.

Virginia Roof Pro holds the appropriate state licensure to handle roof replacement projects of all sizes throughout Lynchburg and the surrounding region. Our license is current, verifiable through DPOR’s public database, and backed by general liability insurance and workers’ compensation coverage—protecting our customers from financial exposure in the event of an on-site incident.

The Risks of Hiring an Unlicensed Roofer in Lynchburg

Unlicensed roofing contractors operate throughout Virginia, often quoting prices that appear attractive on the surface. But the risks are substantial and, in many cases, expensive to undo:

  • Unlicensed work may fail inspections required by the City of Lynchburg’s Department of Community Development
  • Manufacturer warranties on roofing materials are frequently voided when installation is not completed by a licensed professional
  • Homeowner’s insurance claims may be denied if damage results from unpermitted or substandard workmanship
  • Lien liability — if an unlicensed contractor fails to pay suppliers, property liens can be placed against your home
  • No recourse through DPOR’s complaint and enforcement process, which only applies to licensed contractors

The choice of a licensed contractor is, ultimately, a form of consumer protection. It ensures accountability at every stage of the project.

Virginia Roof Pro is fully licensed through Virginia DPOR, carries comprehensive general liability insurance, and provides workers’ compensation coverage for every member of our roofing crews working in Lynchburg.

Licensing, Permits, and Lynchburg Building Codes

Roof replacement projects in Lynchburg typically require a building permit issued by the City’s Community Development office. Only licensed contractors may pull these permits. The permit process ensures a qualified city inspector reviews the completed installation, confirming it meets the Virginia Uniform Statewide Building Code (USBC)—a code that incorporates standards from the International Residential Code (IRC) and is updated periodically to reflect best practices in construction safety.

Virginia Roof Pro manages the entire permit process on behalf of our Lynchburg customers. We file the application, coordinate the inspection schedule, and deliver a final compliance sign-off before we consider any project complete. You never have to navigate city bureaucracy alone.
