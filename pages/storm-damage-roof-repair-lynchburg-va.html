What types of storm damage are most common on Lynchburg roofs?
Lynchburg's position in the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains exposes homes to a wide range of storm threats. The most common damage types include wind-lifted or missing shingles from strong thunderstorm downbursts, hail impacts that bruise asphalt shingles and crack granule coatings, ice dam formation during freeze-thaw cycles in winter, and debris or limb damage from the region's mature hardwood trees. We inspect for all of these after every major weather event.
How do I know if my roof has hidden storm damage after a Lynchburg storm?
Many types of storm damage are not visible from the ground. Signs to watch for include granules from shingles collecting in gutters or downspouts, dark water stains on interior ceilings or attic rafters, daylight visible in your attic, lifted flashing around chimneys or vents, and soft or spongy spots on your roof deck. Virginia Roof Pro provides free comprehensive post-storm roof inspections for Lynchburg homeowners so you never have to guess about the condition of your roof.
How long does a storm damage roof repair typically take in Lynchburg, VA?
The duration depends on the extent of the damage. Minor repairs such as replacing a section of missing shingles or resealing lifted flashing can often be completed in a single day. Moderate repairs involving decking replacement or extensive shingle work typically take one to two days. Full roof replacements due to catastrophic storm damage usually take two to four days depending on the home's size and roof complexity.
