Lynchburg’s Climate and What It Means for Your Roof

Lynchburg averages around 41 inches of rainfall per year, with a good portion of that delivered through intense summer thunderstorms and lingering frontal systems in the fall. The city also averages roughly 10 to 14 inches of snowfall annually, and periods of freezing rain are not uncommon in January and February. This combination creates real challenges for roofing systems that are not properly maintained or installed.

Ice damming is a concern on many of the older homes in areas like Daniel’s Hill and the Daniels Hill area, where lower attic insulation levels and steeper rooflines can lead to ice buildup at the eaves during thaw-refreeze cycles. Summer heat pushes attic temperatures well above ambient outdoor levels, accelerating shingle degradation over time. Properly installed ventilation systems and high-quality shingles rated for Virginia’s climate zone are essential — and both are things we take seriously on every project.
